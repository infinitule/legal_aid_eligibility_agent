# How to apply for free legal aid

> Reference note compiled from NALSA public information and the Legal Services
> Authorities Act, 1987. Indicative — confirm local procedure with your DLSA.

Legal services are delivered through a hierarchy of institutions: the National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) at the top, State Legal Services Authorities (SLSA) in each State, High Court Legal Services Committees (HCLSC), District Legal Services Authorities (DLSA) in each district, and Taluk/Sub-divisional Legal Services Committees (TLSC) at the taluk level. For Supreme Court matters, the Supreme Court Legal Services Committee (SCLSC) is the point of contact.

How a person applies:

1. Approach the nearest legal services institution — most commonly the DLSA, which usually sits in the district court complex. You can also apply to the SLSA, TLSC, or SCLSC depending on where the case is.
2. Apply online at nalsa.gov.in, or through the NALSA Legal Services Mobile App.
3. Call the national legal aid helpline 15100 for guidance.
4. Fill the prescribed application form. A Para-Legal Volunteer (PLV) at the institution helps applicants fill the form free of charge and explains the process.

The application asks for the applicant's identity, address, the category of eligibility relied on, income (if relying on the income ground), and a short description of the legal problem. Supporting documents are attached to prove identity, address, and the ground of eligibility.

After the application is submitted, the Legal Services Authority scrutinises it, checks eligibility under Section 12 and whether there is a prima facie case under Section 13, and — if granted — assigns a panel lawyer to the applicant at no cost. There is no court fee or lawyer's fee for an aided person.

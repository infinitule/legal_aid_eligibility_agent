# What free legal services include

> Reference note compiled from the NALSA (Free and Competent Legal Services)
> Regulations, 2010 and the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987. Indicative.

Free legal services for an eligible person are not limited to a lawyer in court. They typically include:

- Payment of court fees, process fees, and all other charges payable in connection with the legal proceedings.
- The services of a panel lawyer to represent the person in legal proceedings.
- Drafting of legal documents, petitions, appeals, and paperwork connected to the case.
- Obtaining and supplying certified copies of orders, judgments, and other documents in the proceedings.
- Preparation of paper books, including printing and translation of documents, where required.
- Legal advice on any matter, and assistance in pre-litigation stages such as drafting notices.

Legal services also cover mediation and conciliation, help before tribunals and authorities, not only regular courts, and legal awareness. Front Offices at legal services institutions receive walk-in requests and route them.

Quality safeguards apply: panel lawyers are selected on experience, and an aided person may request a change of lawyer for good reason. The person should be kept informed about the progress of their case.

Free legal services can be withdrawn if it is found that the person obtained aid by misrepresentation or fraud, if the person has adequate means, or if the person does not cooperate. Withdrawal is an exception and is decided by the Authority.

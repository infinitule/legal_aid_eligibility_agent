# NALSA schemes for special groups

> Reference note compiled from NALSA public information. Scheme names and details
> are indicative and updated periodically; confirm the current scheme with SLSA/DLSA.

Beyond the general Section 12 categories, NALSA runs targeted schemes to reach vulnerable groups. These commonly include:

- Victims of trafficking and commercial sexual exploitation — legal aid, rescue-and-rehabilitation support, and victim compensation guidance.
- Child-friendly legal services — support for children in conflict with law and child victims, coordinated with Juvenile Justice institutions and Child Welfare Committees.
- Scheme for effective implementation of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act — legal aid and awareness for SC/ST atrocity victims.
- Legal services to persons with mental illness and mental disabilities.
- Legal services for senior citizens — assistance under State schemes for maintenance and welfare.
- Legal services to victims of acid attacks — help with compensation and treatment-related claims.
- Protection and enforcement of the rights of workers in the unorganised sector, and of migrant labour.
- Legal services to disaster victims and to persons in custody, including under-trial review.

Each scheme identifies the target beneficiaries, the kind of help offered (legal aid, awareness, victim compensation, follow-up), and the delivery mechanism through DLSAs and Para-Legal Volunteers. A person who belongs to one of these groups should mention it when applying, because it can establish eligibility and route them to the right support.

Victim compensation is separate from legal aid: under Section 357A of the Code of Criminal Procedure and State victim compensation schemes, victims of certain crimes may claim compensation, and legal services institutions can assist with those claims.

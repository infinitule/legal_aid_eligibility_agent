# Lok Adalats, mediation, and documents to carry

> Reference note compiled from the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 (Chapter VI)

## Lok Adalat and amicable settlement
Lok Adalats are organised by legal services institutions under Sections 19–22 of the Act to settle disputes amicably. A Lok Adalat can take up pending court cases and pre-litigation disputes referred to it. Its award has the status of a decree of a civil court and is final and binding on the parties, with no appeal. There is no court fee for matters settled in a Lok Adalat, and court fee already paid may be refunded. Permanent Lok Adalats handle certain public-utility service disputes. Mediation and conciliation are also offered before matters escalate to litigation.

## Documents commonly required
When applying for legal aid, an applicant generally carries:

- The completed Legal Services Authority application form.
- Proof of identity — Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving Licence.
- Proof of residence or address, to establish which DLSA has jurisdiction.
- Two recent passport-size photographs.
- A self-declaration or affidavit of the facts and of eligibility.

Depending on the ground of eligibility, additional proof is needed: a caste certificate for SC/ST, a disability (UDID) certificate, an FIR or official certificate for disaster or trafficking victims, employment proof for an industrial workman, a custody reference for a person in custody, age proof for a child or senior citizen, and an income certificate, BPL card, or ration card for income-based eligibility.

Matter-specific documents help the panel lawyer prepare — for example, a copy of the FIR or charge-sheet in a criminal case, a marriage certificate and any protection-order copies in a family case, or title deeds and tax receipts in a property dispute. Applicants should carry originals plus one photocopy; a Para-Legal Volunteer can help certify copies.
